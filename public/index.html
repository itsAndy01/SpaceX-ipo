Why don't all funds add SpaceX at the same time?

01
Each index has its own rulebook
CRSP (used by Vanguard) can add stocks within days. The S&P 500 requires a committee vote. Russell reconstitutes on a fixed annual schedule.
02
Float requirements
SpaceX insiders are locked up post-IPO. Until enough shares are freely tradable ("float"), some indexes can't include it — or weight it lower than its market cap suggests.
03
Pre-IPO private access
Active funds like Fidelity Contrafund bought SpaceX shares in private funding rounds years before the IPO. Index funds can't do this — they can only buy listed shares.
04
This site updates daily
After the IPO, fund issuers publish daily holdings CSV files. This site checks them automatically every morning and updates weightings in real time.
